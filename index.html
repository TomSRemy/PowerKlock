Methodology & definitions

This page documents the calculations and statistical methods used across PowerKlock. Each section indicates where the term applies inside the dashboard.

Prices

Cross-zone Bands · Z-score view

Used in: Prices > Day-Ahead > Daily > Cross-zone analysis > Bands view.

The Z-score view normalises every selected zone onto a common dimensionless scale so anomalies pop out regardless of price levels. A zone with prices in €10-€30 range and another in €80-€150 range become directly comparable.

Per-slot baseline

For each zone Z and each 15-min slot s (00:00, 00:15, … 23:45), we collect the prices of that slot across the last N days (7D / 1M / 3M / 6M / YTD / 1Y, default 1M):

prices_s = [p₁, p₂, ..., p_N]

We then compute the per-slot median and per-slot robust sigma:

median_s = P50(prices_s)
σ_s      = (P75(prices_s) − P25(prices_s)) / 1.349

Why a robust sigma

A classical standard deviation squares each residual, so a single price spike (+500 €/MWh during a tight supply event) or a deep negative print (−50 €/MWh during solar surplus) inflates σ disproportionately. After two or three such events in a 30-day window, a "tense but normal" day looks calm (Z ≈ 0.5σ) when it shouldn't.

The interquartile range (IQR = P75 − P25) ignores anything outside the central 50 % of the distribution. Dividing by 1.349 makes the result numerically identical to a classical sigma when the distribution is normal — so the familiar thresholds (|Z| > 2 = anomaly, |Z| > 3 = extreme) still apply. It just stays stable when distributions have fat tails, which is the rule for power prices.

Z-score

Z_s = (today_s − median_s) / σ_s

For today's price at slot s, Z_s tells how many robust standard deviations away from the historical median this price is.

Threshold conventions

|Z|InterpretationVisual flag
< 1Within the typical band—
1 → 2Above normal variability—
2 → 3Statistical anomalyRow highlighted
> 3Extreme outlierRow highlighted, mentioned in Market Read

Per-day Z-score (for the table column "Z")

The table reports a single per-day Z aggregating the 96 per-slot Z values for that zone — taken as the mean of Z_s over all valid slots. This gives a one-number summary of how far the whole day deviates from its baseline.

Worked example

Slot 12:00, last 30 days: 28 values between €30 and €70, plus two outliers (−€20 and €180).

  • median_s = 50 €/MWh
  • Classical σ = 45 €/MWh (dominated by the two outliers)
  • Robust σ via IQR/1.349 = 12 €/MWh
  • If today's 12:00 print is 75 €/MWh:
    • Classical Z = (75 − 50) / 45 = 0.55 → "looks fine"
    • Robust Z = (75 − 50) / 12 = 2.08 → flagged as anomaly

The robust score is the one we trust here, because 75 €/MWh at midday is genuinely unusual relative to the typical solar-driven regime.

Cross-zone Bands · 30D position column

Used in: Prices > Day-Ahead > Daily > Cross-zone analysis > Bands data table.

The "30D position" column shows each zone's today value placed inside the distribution of its last N days (matching the chart period selector). The visual is a compact horizontal boxplot:

  • Thin whiskers extend from the minimum to the maximum observed
  • The blue box marks the interquartile range (P25 to P75 — the central 50 % of days)
  • The vertical tick inside the box is the median (P50)
  • The coloured bullet is today's price

A bullet sitting inside the box is "in line"; one against a whisker tip means today is at or beyond the 30-day extreme. The companion "Pctile" column gives the same information as a percentile rank (P10 = bottom decile, P50 = median, P90 = top decile).

Drill-down · historical envelope

Used in: Prices > Day-Ahead > Daily > Day-Ahead board > row drill-down > Band selector.

The same per-slot distribution is computed for the single drilled-down zone, then plotted as three overlays on the intraday chart:

  • Range 0-100 % — light fill spanning P0 to P100 (the full historical range slot by slot)
  • Typical 10-90 % — stronger fill spanning P10 to P90 (the typical band)
  • Median — dashed line at P50

Today's curve sitting outside the Typical 10-90 % band means the day deviates from its recent baseline at that slot. The "Today vs band" sentence in the Market Read banner reports the day-level percentile, the value vs median, and the slot with the largest divergence.

General terms

Auction date vs delivery date

Day-Ahead prices are auctioned the day before delivery (the EPEX/Nord Pool auction closes at 12:00 CET on day D-1 for delivery on day D). Throughout PowerKlock, dates refer to the delivery date by default. Historical CSV filenames in data/history/daily/ use the auction date by convention.

Peak / Off-peak

European convention used here: peak = 08:00-20:00 (12 hours), off-peak = 00:00-08:00 + 20:00-24:00 (12 hours). Peak/Off-pk ratio < 1 is an "inverted profile" (often driven by midday solar saturating the market). A baseline value around 1.30x means peak is roughly 30 % more expensive than off-peak.

J-1, J, J+1

French market shorthand. J = today (the displayed delivery day). J-1 = the previous delivery day. J+1 = tomorrow. In the chart legends, J-1 appears as the day-name (e.g. "Wed 20 May") of the previous delivery day.

Time zone

All times are CET / CEST as published by ENTSO-E Transparency. Slot times in the breakdown table (00:00, 00:15, …) are local delivery-time clock readings, with daylight-saving transitions handled by the source data.
